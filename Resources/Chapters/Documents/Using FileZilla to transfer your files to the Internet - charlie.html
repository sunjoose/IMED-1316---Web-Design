Use FileZilla to transfer files to the Internet

  1. Open FileZilla by clicking FileZilla.exe in your IMED1316 folder (not FileZilla.zip) (Can't see the .zip or .exe extensions? Click here and select which version of Windows you're using.)
  2. Host: ftp://web.blinn.edu/alpha
  3. Username: a19
  4. Password: IMed1316 (case sensitive, no space)
  5. Click Quickconnect.

Submit your assignment to the D2L Drop Box using Filezilla.

4. Upload your folder containing your completed assignment using FileZilla.

  1. In the lower right hand box, click a19. (Actually, you'll click your OWN username.)
  2. In the lower left hand box, find the folder you wish to upload. 
  3. Drag that folder from the left hand box to the right hand box.
  4. In the right-hand box, right click on that folder name.
  5. Select Copy URL to clipboard.

5. Open the link in Chrome. 

  1. Paste the link into a new tab in Chrome. Hit ENTER.
  2. Replace the first three letters (FTP) with HTTP. Hit ENTER again. You should see something like this:

6. Paste the link into the eCampus drop box.

  1. In eCampus, in the Drop Box for the assignment, upload a generic .TXT file. Please do not be clever and upload a music, video, or image file. They take up too much server space. (How to create a generic.txt file to use for all eCampus submissions.)
  2. Click the Comments box.
  3. Paste the URL into the D2L comment box. 
  4. Place your cursor after HTML and hit ENTER.
  5. NOW it should be underlined.
  6. Try the link and see if it takes you to the right place.  Look it over to make sure it looks good enough for a grade.
  7. If so, you are ready to successfully submit your work for grading. 

Why you need a flash drive even if you have a laptop

If you need help with your assignment and come to me for assistance, you will need all of your work on that flash drive. You and I will pop it into my computer to view the trouble.

Why you have to use the Chrome browser

There are many variables among computers, including platforms (Windows/Mac/Android) and browsers (Chrome/Edge/IE/Safari/Firefox). When students have problems and ask me for help, I can eliminate this variable from our troubleshooting.
