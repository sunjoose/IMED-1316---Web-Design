Student Learning Objectives

  • Identify how the Internet functions with specific attention to the World Wide Web and file transfer.
  • Apply design techniques in the creation and optimization of graphics and other embedded elements.
  • Demonstrate the use of World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) formatting and layout standards.
  • Design, create, test, and maintain a web site.

Design Layout

OBJECTIVE: Plan the layout of the elements on your pages.

SCORING: The design layout is a ten-point assignment. Instructor will guide student with suggestions; student may change it as much as they wish.

REFERENCE: To see two very different examples of what a design layout can look like, see Figure 3-3 on page 142 and Figure 4-2 on page 225.

THEME: You should have a unifying theme for your website. 

DESCRIPTION: The design layout (also called a wireframe or story board) should show each page of your five-page website. Submit Word, Adobe Illustrator, Power Point, or a scanned dark sharpie sketch.

  • Some elements need to be identical on every page. These are the BANNER, NAVIGATION BAR, and FOOTER.
  • Consider a subtopic for each page, and label the page with that.
  • One page is the Index. One page is the Summary.  The other pages may be whatever you wish.
  • At a minimum, on each page, mark the layout of the header, banner, nav bar, sections, asides, footers, youtube video link, audio, linked image, logo, .MP3, etc.

SUBMIT this to the Project Brainstorming Discussion Forum for peer review and ideas.  

Summary Page - 25% of your project grade

OBJECTIVES:  (1) Avoid plagiarism by giving credit for elements used in your web site that are not your own.  (2) Type a description, analysis, and problems experienced in the process you went through in developing your website. 

DESCRIPTION: The Summary page is the final page of your five-page website. It has the same elements as every page (Banner, Navigation Bar, and Footer.) Other than that, it does not contain graphics unless you wish you use graphic images such as you see in this document.  That is not necessary; it will not improve your grade.

Now that you have chosen your topic, create a blank HTM file. Name this page summary.htm.  Do not worry about the formatting for now; just include the links you use. When you finish the rest of your website, you can copy the common elements and CSS style link into this document, and it should flow nicely with your website.

Make two sections or headings on the page. Label them Analysis and Links. For the first section (labeled ANALYSIS), copy and paste the following questions AS TEXT into the page.

  1. How did you change copied or borrowed material to make it more original?
  2. What difficulties did you encounter in building the website? How did you overcome them?
  3. What part of the website are you most proud of? Why?
  4. If you had more time, what would you change?

Answer these questions as you work through the project.  Post your answers to the summary page. 

For the second section (labeled LINKS), post the links to anything you used in the creation of this project, including online tutorials, images, style sheets, banner generators, thumbnail generators, javascript libraries, etc. Make sure the links are attractive - not underlined, with a CSS style for the hover or rollover effect.

You must give credit for your sources. Every link, product, software, or site mentioned that you did not create from scratch must be linked. GOOGLE IS NOT A SOURCE. GOOGLE IS A SEARCH ENGINE THAT LEADS YOU TO THE SOURCE.

Website Elements

The structural elements of your website must include a banner with a logo, a navigation bar, an aside, sections, articles, and a footer.  The banner, footer, and nav bar should be consistent across all pages of the site, even the summary page.

Banner

  • The banner is uniformly placed and appears on every page.
  • Alignment stays the same when window is resized.
  • Banner contains a logo which links to the home page.

You can use any image editing program you wish, such as Photo Shop or Paint, or google “online banner generator”. Make sure you include the link to the app or program you use in your Summary page. 

Navigation bar

  • The navigation bar may be a mapped image or a list.
  • If may be horizontal or vertical.
  • All links and lists must NOT be underlined in the passive state, but they need to somehow change in the hover mode.
  • All links and lists must either have no bullet or a decorative image bullet.

Footer

  • The footer must be located uniformly on every page
  • The footer must contain your first name, which, when clicked, loads either MY email address, OR your email address scrambled as in the javascript chapter (so that you do not get spammed.) It should also include the Blinn physical address (2423 Blinn Blvd., Bryan, TX  77802)

Individual Pages

There must be five linked pages, INCLUDING THE INDEX PAGE AND THE SUMMARY PAGE, loaded to the server, the link submitted to eCampus.
TEST YOUR LINKS in the lab well before your presentation! The following items must be present.

Each page should have :

  • A title that displays in the browser tab
  • Design that is symmetrical and consistent
  • Font that is easy to read
  • Background color and font color that complement each other
  • No typos, misspellings, or grammatical errors
  • All links and lists must not be underlined in the passive state, but they need to somehow change in the hover mode.
  • All links and lists must either have no bullet or a decorative image bullet.
  • All images must have alt text.

The following elements must also be present and working within the website

  • An internally linked image
  • An externally linked image
  • A video OR
  • Audio that plays in the background
  • A form
  • A floated aside
  • At least one separate CSS style sheet that dictates the style of the site
  • A table – not used for layout purposes
